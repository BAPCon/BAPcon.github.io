Orlando Housing Data Science



Purpose

After seeing numerous news reports about large companies like BlackRock and Zillow buying up residential property, I thought it would be interesting to explore the level of corporate ownership in Orange County, Florida.

I couldn't find any straightforward statistics regarding this topic, so I decided to source the data myself.

Approach

Most counties have extremely accesible tax appraiser offices, often with web applications to get information on specific properties.

By respectfully interfacing with Orange County's backend, I first wrote a program to identify every address first. With a built-in 'suggested address' feature, it was quick to identify roughly 100,000 properties.

A pipeline for taking an address and getting the uuid associated with it, pulling the property records, and extracting the various datapoints within the records was built.

At first, I spent a lot of time seeking to identify corporate entities as opposed to citizens. Ultimately, this was pointless, and I instead just sorted by the number of properties associated with each owner.

Conclusions

  1. A few large corporations owned thousands of houses.
  2. Corporate-owned properties were clustered in low-income areas of Orange County, and heavily clustered near the University of Central Florida
  3. Massive suburban developments were often owned entirely by one corporation, though often in recently constructed developments
  4. Ascertaining ownership was obfuscated by corporations using variations of their name. Ie: HouseCorp A, HouseCorp East, HouseCorp C
